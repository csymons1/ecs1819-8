Sylvia Rivera



Who She Influenced

The Children of the STAR Initiative

STAR (Street Transvestite Action Revolutionaries) was a street activist orginization founded by Sylvia Rivera with the goal to fight for equal rights and give housing and food to homeless transgender and queer youth.

Marsha P. Johnson

Marsha P. Johnson was a close friend of Rivera, a transgender woman who co-founded STAR and fought alognside Rivera in their fight for equal rights, another prominent figure at Stonewall.

Profile


Michael Bronski

Bronski is an American writer and professor of the Practice in Media and Activism at Harvard University, he currently teaches in the women, gender and sexuality program. He wrote "A Queer History of the United States" and was always inspired by Rivera's work and dedication.

Profile

Stonewall

In 1969, at the Stonewall Inn (a gay bar), dozens of members of the LGBTQ+ community stood up against the police force who broke in on a routine bar raid. This resistance quickly decended into violence, with a nineteen year old Sylvia Rivera on the front lines. This was one of the major beginnings of Sylvia's career in activism, as she stood face to face with the police and fought for what she believed in.
